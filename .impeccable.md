# Impeccable Design Context — Vigilant

## Design Context

### Users
Everyday consumers who bought products — shampoo, supplements, cleaning supplies — and don't yet know some of those products are tied to class action lawsuits. They arrive curious or mildly anxious, and the app's job is to transform that into a feeling of discovery and empowerment: *"I was owed this money and now I know."* They're not lawyers, not activists — just people who feel the system was quietly working against them and want a fair shake. They use this during a focused session, likely on a laptop, in daylight hours.

### Brand Personality
**Vigilant. Empowering. Resourceful.**

The brand is an advocate, not an alarm. It doesn't panic-sell health risk — it surfaces quiet truths and puts the user in control. The tone is confident and clear, like a smart friend who did the research for you. Never clinical, never legal-ish, never fear-mongering.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Reference**: Apple.com — editorial whitespace, large purposeful typography, storytelling through hierarchy, premium polish with restraint. Products are revealed as desirable objects; here, *findings* are revealed as valuable discoveries.
- **Anti-references**: Generic health dashboards (grids of colored cards with icons), ambulance-chaser law firm websites (aggressive red CTAs), wellness/spa aesthetics (soft pastels, leaf motifs).
- **Theme**: Light — this is a daytime, focused research session. Clean, confident.
- **Colors**: Evolve from the current teal-green + amber. Move toward a richer, more authoritative palette. Deep forest green (not medical teal) as the primary — trustworthy, growth-oriented. Warm amber gold as accent — value, discovery, reward. Neutrals tinted slightly warm, away from the cold blue-gray defaults.
- **Typography**: Editorial and confident. A distinguished display face for headlines that make declarative statements. A refined, slightly humanist body face for reading comfort. No system defaults, no reflex fonts.

### Design Principles
1. **Revelation over alarm** — findings are discoveries the user is owed, not diagnoses they should fear. Every data point is framed as empowerment.
2. **Editorial hierarchy** — typography and whitespace carry the design. One strong headline, clear secondary, supporting detail. Never four things competing for attention.
3. **Apple-grade choreography** — transitions are purposeful and sequenced, not sprinkled. The flow of information has a cadence.
4. **Empower through progressive disclosure** — lead with the headline finding (you may be owed $X), reveal chemical detail and legal nuance on demand. Don't front-load complexity.
5. **Premium without pretense** — feels like a high-end consumer app, not a corporate compliance tool or a startup MVP. Every border-radius, every shadow, every spacing decision is deliberate.
